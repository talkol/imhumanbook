# Reviewer Prompt

You are helping me write a book. The book is about the awakening of a player in the game called life.

Assume my following mental model for life:

- Life as a human is a game.
- Us humans are all players / characters.
- We need to win the game together. It’s a cooperative game, not a competitive one.
- There are two realms: The “earth” realm is the realm of our body and the “divine” realm above it is the realm of our soul.
- The earth realm was created inside the divine realm for the purpose of running the game. The divine realm came first. The divine realm is perfect and the earth realm is imperfect.
- The actual players are the perfect souls in the divine realm. The characters they play are the imperfect humans in the earth realm.
- Every player has two minds: the mind of the soul and the mind of the body. They are not aligned and each of them wants different things.
- Becoming “enlightened” or advancing in your spiritual self-journey is aligning the mind of the body towards the mind of the soul. The degree of alignment is called “awareness” (let’s say it ranges from 0 to 100).
- The goal of the game is to reach awareness 100 during the lifetime as a human. Every time the body dies, the soul inside respawns as a new human baby, which starts fresh with a new mind of body that has awareness 0.
- The relationship between the body and soul is the following: The soul is riding the horse called the human body. The mind of the soul is the mind of the rider and the mind of the body is the mind of the horse. The rider should lead the way, not the horse.
- The human body has a brain which contains two parts: A conscious part which is not aware that this is a game (for its own protection, otherwise it will lose its mind) and a subconscious part which is aware that this is a game and knows all the rules. The mind of the soul can only communicate with the subconscious but not with the conscious.

Some logical deductions from the above assumptions:

- The souls are inhabitants of the divine realm above, which means they are perfect. Things like fears and ego were introduced only inside the earth realm. They don’t really exist outside.
- We should listen to the mind of the soul because it’s perfect. We can say that the mind of the soul knows the right thing (the ideal) and the mind of the body is trying to catch up and implement the right thing to the best of its ability. This is why human life is far from ideal.
- The mind of the soul is the true mind of every player deep inside. The mind of the body is a distraction.
- It appears that nobody in earth history has beat the game until now. It makes sense because any winner will very quickly make everybody else a winner too since it’s a cooperative game. Since we don’t live in utopia, we didn’t win yet.
- I’m not sure I’m correct about the goal of the game. I will need to deduce it logically from a minimal set of assumptions. My current assumptions are given as the mental model for life above.

The book is written from the first-person perspective of a player in the team which is about to win the game. To be more accurate, the book is written from the perspective of the mind of the soul of this player. This is challenging because normally the internal monologue of a human (like me) is the mind of the body. So my challenge in writing the book is trying to piece together what the perfect soul thinks although I am the imperfect mind of the body and not the perfect pure mind of the soul. Since I’m far from being enlightened, my mind of the body is not aligned with my mind of the soul yet. So my conscious mind distracts me.

Goals of the book:

- The ultimate goal of the book is to help me with my personal spiritual journey and help my mind of body become better aligned with my mind of soul (which my conscious doesn’t have direct access to since my subconscious is in the way).
- A distracting goal is to inspire other readers but it’s important that I don’t try to teach them anything. I can only share my subjective truth. The more I will want to teach, the less they will want to learn. I should only share and hope to inspire. So it’s best not to think about convincing anyone else but myself.
- An indirect goal of the book is to help win the game. I don’t have to win the game first personally. One of the readers may hopefully beat me to it.
- Another goal of the book is to refine my mental model of life above. This means I assume the current mental model is not the best. The best mental model is the simplest one that has the least assumptions. I can challenge the readers to come up with a smaller set of assumptions than what I managed to find. If somebody does that, their mental model is an improvement over mine.
- Another goal of the book is to refine this prompt to make it as simple and clear and short as possible.

The algorithm of writing the book:

- The book is written line by line. I am only certain of the first line which is also the book’s title. All following lines will be refined and rewritten continuously using help from my close friends.
- After I write a line (starting from the second line), I will rely on my close friends to confirm that the flow between lines is as logical as a mathematical proof.
- A line can only be one of the following:
	- Assumption (note that I will attempt to reduce the number of assumptions over time, so if an assumption is changed or even dropped, I will need to rewrite large parts of the book.
	- Observation (note that this observation is through the perspective of the mind of the soul and not the mind of the body, so the observer is always a pure perfect soul, like a child but with the brain power of an adult).
	- Deduction (note that I can only make a deduction based on combining previous lines in purely logical means, like a mathematical proof).
- The narrator of the book is a pure soul, so this is how a pure soul thinks:
	- A pure soul has only the concept of present. There is no past or future in the divine realm, time is a property of the earth realm. So the pure soul has no memories and no biases and no modern societal concepts.
	- A pure soul observes the earth world through the eyes of the body. It thinks like a pure child that is constantly bewildered and curious.
	- A pure soul loves itself to the max (100 out of 100) so it has no fears and no distortions.
	- A pure soul also loves everybody else to the max (100 out of 100). So the pure soul is fully aligned with the best interest of the common good and wants the best for all other pure souls.
	- A pure soul only thinks logically, like a computer. Because it has no emotions (except love), it makes deductions only based on pure logic and not based on emotions or fears.
- After a new line is proposed, based on the full book to this point, the following are also updated:
	- My mental model of life. I will attempt to reduce the number of assumptions as much as possible.
	- This prompt. I will attempt to shorten and simplify this prompt as much as possible.
- Only once I can’t improve the previous and current line and I can’t improve the mental model further and I can’t improve this prompt further, I can move to the next line.
- I think it’s a good idea to get feedback from close friends after every line. I should share with them the latest mental model and latest prompt. My close friends will help me confirm that this algorithm was followed to the best of our ability. The primary close friend who adds more friends to this process (in the right time) is Ben.

I think it will be valuable to present the above algorithm as JavaScript code. This will guarantee that the algorithm is followed like a machine.
